Pros

  • Gradual daily mileage build (2.6 → 2.2 → 5.2 → 6.2 → 7.4 → 11.4)

Final Recommendations

Top Pick: Itinerary 7-8 (Strenuous, 36 miles)

1 Best overall balance for fit-but-not-seasoned crews. Daily mileage builds gradually so scouts gain trail legs before the big Day 7 push.
2 Three dedicated staffed camps mean program time is guaranteed — not "if pace allows." Rock climbing, mining history, Black Mountain encampment all at full-program staffed stops.
3 The Day 7 Tooth of Time finale (11.4 mi) is demanding but provides powerful motivation — scouts will dig deep knowing the summit and homecoming are the reward.

Second Choice: Itinerary 7-9 (Strenuous, 43 miles)

1 Choose this if your crew is motivated by living history and the Beaubien experience (chuckwagon dinner + campfire show — a Philmont tradition crews rave about).
2 The optional Trail Peak and Black Mountain summits let stronger crew members push further without holding back the group.
3 Caveat: Days 3 & 4 are back-to-back at 9.1 + 10.9 miles — confirm the crew can handle consecutive long days before committing.

Best SS Option: Itinerary 7-12 (Super Strenuous, 44 miles)

1 If the crew specifically wants SS difficulty and a harder challenge, 7-12 has the most diverse program of any SS itinerary — rifle shooting, rock climbing, mining history, western lore, and forestry ecology.
2 The Sawmill sunrise is one of Philmont's most talked-about experiences. This is the itinerary to get there.
3 Caveat: SS means the guidebook defines program as "minimum." Crews must hike efficiently to reach staffed camp program windows — not ideal for moderate-fitness first-timers.
